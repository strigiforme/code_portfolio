Archetypes

The use of archetypes enables the construction of an identity that is easily recognizable, and has innate characteristics that are universal to all. Archetypes have been employed with success at companies, in literature, and in self discovery. By applying archetypes to a brand or design, you gain consistency, personality, and you may better understand your strengths and shortcomings.

My Archetypes

My personality most strongly features three archetypes: The Creator, The Hero, and The Sage. While a person is a blend of many Archetypes, a brand should only consist of 1-2 archetypes in order to be more recognizeable. As such, I will be using The Creator and The Hero archetypes for my brand. I have chosen these archetypes because they describe me the best, and also are the attributes I value most. I will discuss both these archetypes and how they will inform my brand.

1. The Creator

Ever since I was young, I have felt the desire and want to create unique things of my own design. Creating is a central part of my identity and life goals. The Creator archetype has helped me reify this desire and how I can find more fulfillment from it. My goal as a creator is to become masterful at creating in many ways. This desire for mastery also is a facet of the hero archetype.

My primary objective as a creator is to be able to do as I please unimpeded by restriction. The only restrictions I find arise in the nature of the work, and I find great pleasure in overcoming these challenges.

Key attributes of the creator archetype:

  • + Aesthetic/Assiduous Design
  • + Expressive/Imaginative Ideas
  • + Strong Artistic/Technical skillset
  • - Perfectionism
  • - Naivety

Design Decisions

I intend to incorporate the Creator archetype in my design via the following:

Thoughtful Design: All elements that are associated with my brand should have their design carefully planned and look intentional. This speaks to devotion and skill as a creator.

Playful Design: An element of expressiveness and playfulness should be present in the design. This helps combat perceived aggression in design, and conveys expressivity and freedom to be imaginative.

2. The Hero

The Hero archetype embodies heroes from stories, athletes, leaders, and masters of fields. They overcome challenge and adversity in order to better themselves. Heroes are bold, powerful, and striking. For me, The Hero archetype is strongly associated with my goals as The Creator, where my desire is to overcome challenge and demonstrate mastery as a Creator is my primary purpose.

Heroes are also known for their self-sacrifice and loyalty to others. These attributes are intrinsic to my beliefs, but I struggle with determining how much self sacrifice is too much, where I may stretch myself too thin.

Key attributes of the Hero archetype:

  • + Brave, bold brand design
  • + High determination
  • + Competence in field
  • - Arrogance
  • - Perceived as Aggressive
  • +/- Doesn't know when to give up

Design Decisions

I intend to incorporate the Hero archetype in my design via the following:

Bold Design: The Hero archetype should be expressed via bold and powerful design. A strong statement should be made in order to convey power, determination, and competence.
